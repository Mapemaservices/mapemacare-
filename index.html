About Mapema Care

Transforming communities through sustainable reforestation

Our Mission

Mapema Care is a Kenya-based NGO dedicated to combating climate change by planting trees with local communities. We partner with smallholder farmers to transform their land into thriving forests that capture carbon dioxide, restore ecosystems, and create sustainable livelihoods.

Our Vision

We envision a world where communities and ecosystems thrive together, creating a sustainable future through reforestation and responsible environmental stewardship.

Our Founder's Story

James Kariuki, Founder & CEO

"Growing up in rural Kenya, I witnessed firsthand the devastating effects of deforestation on our communities. Our streams dried up, soil eroded, and livelihoods disappeared. After studying environmental science, I knew I had to take action."

"In 2018, I founded Mapema Care with a simple mission: partner with farmers to plant trees that restore ecosystems while providing sustainable income. 'Mapema' means 'early' in Swahili - we believe it's never too early to act against climate change."

"Today, what started with 5 farmers has grown to a movement engaging hundreds of communities across Kenya. Our model proves that environmental restoration and economic empowerment can go hand-in-hand."

The Science Behind Carbon Capture

Trees are nature's most efficient carbon capture technology. Through photosynthesis, trees absorb CO₂ from the atmosphere and store carbon in their biomass. The amount of carbon sequestered depends on tree species, growth rate, and environmental conditions.

Our approach:

  • Planting native species optimized for carbon capture
  • Strategic placement to maximize sequestration potential
  • Rigorous monitoring and measurement protocols
  • Third-party verification of carbon storage

On average, each tree we plant captures approximately 22 kg of CO₂ per year. Over a 20-year lifespan, this adds up to significant carbon removal from the atmosphere.

Frequently Asked Questions

How are carbon credits calculated?

We use internationally recognized methodologies to calculate carbon sequestration based on tree species, growth rates, and biomass accumulation. Each credit represents one ton of CO₂ removed from the atmosphere.

What verification standards do you use?

All our projects are verified by independent third parties using Verra's VM0007 methodology and Gold Standard's GS4GG framework.

How long until we receive our credits?

Credits are issued annually after verification of tree growth and survival rates. Initial verification occurs 12 months after planting.

Can we visit our sponsored forest?

Yes! We organize annual stakeholder visits where you can meet the farmers and see your impact firsthand.

What ESG benefits will we receive?

Partners receive comprehensive impact reports for ESG disclosures, marketing rights to our certification marks, and case studies for sustainability reporting.

How to Qualify

We work with smallholder farmers across Kenya who meet the following criteria:

  • Own or have secure access to at least 1/2 acre of land
  • Commit to maintaining trees for a minimum of 10 years
  • Attend our sustainable agroforestry training program
  • Allow regular monitoring of tree growth and health
